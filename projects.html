Projects

Visual Question Answering

We study different problems in visual question answering (VQA). Our main interest is on how to incorporate external knowledge in VQA tasks to address complex questions beyond the image content. We created a dataset for knowledge-based VQA in videos, KnowIT VQA. To know more about the project, please visit the project website.

Automatic Art Analysis

In this project, we aim to study paintings and their underlying semantics. We introduce the SemArt dataset, a collection of paintings annotated with artistic attributes and natural language comments. We perform experiments on multiple language and vision baselines in a multi-modal retrieval task, showing that current models still lags well behind human accuracy. More details in the SemArt web.

Recently, we showed that artistic attribute recognition and semantic art understanding can be improved when incorporating context-aware information from knowledge graphs.

Image-to-Video Retrieval

In this project, we address the problem of identifying a video scene in large-scale collections of videos by using only static images. We propose two models to exploit temporal redundancies in video, one based on binary features and another one based on CNNs and RNNs. We also present a framework to find interesting fashion items in videos based on image-to-video retrieval.


Parking Detection

This project addresses the problem of detecting and classifying parking spaces in either car parks or in the street using 3D reconstruction techniques and feature analysis. The project was conducted at G@TV group at Universidad Politecnica de Madrid and it was funded by the Spanish Industry, Tourism and Commerce Council and the ERDF (reference TSI-020100-2011-57).

Heart Rate Estimation

My first computer vision project conducted at Gradiant as my final undergrad project. We implemented a system to estimate the heart rate of a person by using facial video information.
